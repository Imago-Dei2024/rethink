General Questions

What services does ReThink Mental Health offer?

ReThink Mental Health offers a comprehensive range of mental health services, including:

  • Adult and pediatric psychiatric evaluation, treatment, and medication management
  • Specialized care for First Responders, Frontline Healthcare workers, and Military personnel
  • Ketamine therapy for treatment-resistant conditions
  • Spravato (esketamine) intranasal treatment
  • Genetic testing and comprehensive diagnostic services
  • QbTesting for ADHD
  • Addiction treatment
  • Wellness services including micronutrient injections and nutrition counseling

For more detailed information about our services, please visit our Services page.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment in several ways:

  • Call our office at (303) 555-1234 during business hours (Monday-Friday, 8am-6pm)
  • Use our online appointment request form on our Contact page
  • Email us at info@rethinkmentalhealth.com

For new patients, we'll need some basic information and will ask you to complete intake forms prior to your first appointment. We strive to accommodate urgent needs and will work to schedule you as soon as possible.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

For your first appointment, please bring:

  • A valid photo ID
  • Your insurance card (if using insurance)
  • A list of current medications and dosages
  • Any relevant medical records or previous psychiatric evaluations
  • Completed intake forms (which will be sent to you after scheduling)
  • Payment for your copay or self-pay amount

It's also helpful to bring a list of questions or concerns you'd like to discuss with your provider.

How long are appointments?

Appointment lengths vary based on the type of service:

  • Initial psychiatric evaluations: 60-90 minutes
  • Medication management follow-ups: 15-30 minutes
  • Ketamine therapy sessions: 2-3 hours (including preparation and recovery)
  • Spravato treatment sessions: 2-2.5 hours (including monitoring period)
  • Diagnostic testing: Varies based on the specific tests
  • Nutrition consultations: 45-60 minutes

Your provider will let you know the expected duration when you schedule your appointment.

Do you offer telehealth appointments?

Yes, we offer telehealth appointments for many of our services, including psychiatric evaluations, medication management, and follow-up visits. Telehealth provides a convenient option for patients who prefer to receive care from home or who have transportation or mobility challenges.

However, some services require in-person visits, including:

  • Ketamine therapy
  • Spravato treatment
  • Wellness injections
  • Certain diagnostic tests

Our staff can help determine whether telehealth is appropriate for your specific needs.

What is your cancellation policy?

We understand that circumstances may arise that require you to cancel or reschedule an appointment. We request at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations (less than 24 hours' notice) or missed appointments may be subject to a fee, which is not covered by insurance.

For ketamine therapy and Spravato treatment appointments, we require 48 hours' notice due to the extended time reserved for these sessions.

If you need to cancel or reschedule, please call our office as soon as possible at (303) 555-1234.

Ketamine Therapy Questions

What conditions can ketamine therapy help with?

Ketamine therapy has shown promising results for several mental health conditions, particularly those that haven't responded well to traditional treatments. Conditions that may benefit from ketamine therapy include:

  • Treatment-resistant depression
  • Major depressive disorder
  • Bipolar depression
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Certain chronic pain conditions

Ketamine is particularly notable for its rapid onset of action, with many patients experiencing improvement in symptoms within hours or days, rather than the weeks often required for traditional antidepressants to take effect.

How does ketamine therapy work?

Ketamine works differently than traditional antidepressants. While most antidepressants primarily affect serotonin, norepinephrine, or dopamine systems, ketamine acts on the glutamate system—the most abundant neurotransmitter in the brain.

Specifically, ketamine blocks NMDA receptors and activates AMPA receptors, which leads to increased levels of glutamate. This triggers a cascade of events that ultimately:

  • Promotes the growth of new neural connections (synaptogenesis)
  • Increases brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), which supports neuron health
  • Reduces inflammation in the brain
  • Disrupts established patterns of negative thinking

These mechanisms help explain why ketamine can provide rapid relief from depression and other mental health conditions, even in cases where other treatments have failed.

What happens during a ketamine infusion session?

A ketamine infusion session at ReThink Mental Health typically follows these steps:

  1. Preparation: You'll arrive at our office and be taken to a private treatment room designed for comfort. We'll check your vital signs and review any changes in your health or medications.
  2. IV Placement: A small IV will be placed in your arm or hand by our experienced medical staff.
  3. Comfortable Setting: You'll recline in a comfortable chair. You can bring headphones with calming music if you wish, or we can provide them.
  4. Infusion: The ketamine will be administered slowly through the IV over about 40-60 minutes. The dose is carefully calculated based on your weight and adjusted according to your response.
  5. Monitoring: Throughout the infusion, our medical team will monitor your vital signs and check in on your comfort level.
  6. Experience: During the infusion, you may experience a sense of dissociation or floating, changes in perception, or a dreamlike state. These experiences are normal and part of the treatment process.
  7. Recovery: After the infusion is complete, you'll remain in our office for about 30-60 minutes until the immediate effects subside.
  8. Going Home: You must have someone drive you home after the treatment, as you should not drive for the remainder of the day.

Our team is present throughout the entire process to ensure your safety and comfort.

How many ketamine treatments will I need?

The typical initial course of ketamine therapy consists of six infusions over a two to three-week period (usually 2-3 infusions per week). This initial series helps to establish the therapeutic effect.

After the initial series, maintenance treatments are often needed to sustain the benefits. The frequency of maintenance treatments varies widely among individuals:

  • Some patients may need a single booster every 2-4 weeks
  • Others may go 1-3 months between treatments
  • Some patients may achieve long-term relief with just the initial series

We work with each patient to determine the optimal treatment schedule based on their response and needs. Our goal is to provide lasting relief with the minimum necessary frequency of treatments.

Is ketamine therapy safe?

Ketamine has been used as an anesthetic in medical settings for over 50 years and has a well-established safety profile when administered by medical professionals. At ReThink Mental Health, ketamine therapy is:

  • Administered by trained medical professionals
  • Provided at sub-anesthetic doses that are significantly lower than those used for anesthesia
  • Conducted with continuous monitoring of vital signs
  • Preceded by a thorough medical screening to identify any contraindications

Common side effects during treatment may include:

  • Feeling of dissociation or floating
  • Mild nausea (which can be treated if needed)
  • Temporary increase in blood pressure
  • Dizziness
  • Blurred vision

These effects typically resolve shortly after the infusion ends. Ketamine therapy is not appropriate for everyone, and we conduct a thorough evaluation to determine if it's a suitable option for each patient.

How do I know if I'm a good candidate for ketamine therapy?

Good candidates for ketamine therapy typically include individuals who:

  • Have been diagnosed with depression, PTSD, OCD, or certain other mental health conditions
  • Have tried multiple traditional treatments without adequate relief
  • Are not experiencing active psychosis
  • Do not have uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • Do not have a history of substance abuse (though this is evaluated on a case-by-case basis)
  • Are not pregnant or breastfeeding

The best way to determine if ketamine therapy is right for you is to schedule a consultation with one of our providers. During this evaluation, we'll review your medical history, current symptoms, previous treatments, and discuss whether ketamine therapy might be beneficial for your specific situation.
